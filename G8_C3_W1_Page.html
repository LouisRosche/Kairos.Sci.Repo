Grade 8 | Cycle 3 Week 1

The Cheetah-Gazelle Arms Race: Connecting Physics to Natural Selection

Week Overview

This week, you will discover how physics principles (forces, acceleration, mass) drive natural selection in predator-prey relationships. Through the cheetah-gazelle "arms race," you will learn that survival is not just about speed - it is about the physics of escape!

Primary Standard

MS-LS4-4 - Construct an explanation based on evidence that describes how genetic variations of traits in a population increase some individuals' probability of surviving and reproducing in a specific environment.

Spiral Standards (Cycle 2)

MS-PS2-1 - Apply Newton's Third Law to design a solution to a problem involving the motion of two colliding objects.

MS-PS2-2 - Plan an investigation to provide evidence that the change in an object's motion depends on the sum of the forces on the object and the mass of the object (F=ma).

Learning Targets

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  1. Apply Newton's Third Law to explain force interactions in predator-prey collisions
  2. Use F=ma to explain why smaller prey can escape larger predators
  3. Describe how trait variation leads to differential survival in populations
  4. Connect physics principles to natural selection mechanisms

Disciplinary Core Ideas (DCI)

LS4.B Natural Selection: Trait variation affects survival probability

PS2.A Forces and Motion: F=ma determines acceleration from contact forces

Crosscutting Concepts (CCC)

Cause and Effect: Mass differences cause acceleration differences, which cause survival differences

Systems and System Models: Population changes emerge from individual interactions
