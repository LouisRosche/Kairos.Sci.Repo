üìö Step 1: What is Energy?

Big Idea:

Energy is the ability to make things happen or cause change.

Energy can:

  • Make things move (kinetic energy)
  • Be stored for later (potential energy)
  • Make things hotter (thermal energy)
  • Make light (light energy)

üñêÔ∏è Quick Activity:

Rub your hands together quickly for 10 seconds. What do you feel?

Your movement energy changed into thermal (heat) energy!

‚úì Check Understanding:

Student, can you give me one example of energy causing change?

(Wait for response. Acceptable: pushing a ball, heating water, turning on a light, etc.)

üìö Step 2: Energy Moves (Transfer)

Big Idea:

Energy can move from one place to another. This is called "transfer."

Example: Hot Spoon in Cold Water

  HOT SPOON ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí COLD WATER
  (loses heat)      (gains heat)

  Energy moves FROM hot TO cold
  until they're the same temperature

üñêÔ∏è Quick Activity:

Touch your desk, then touch your face. Which feels cooler?

The desk feels cooler because energy is transferring FROM your warm hand TO the cooler desk!

‚úì Check Understanding:

If I put an ice cube in warm water, which direction does energy move?

See answer

Energy moves FROM the warm water TO the ice cube. That's why the water gets cooler and the ice melts!

üìö Step 3: Energy IN vs Energy OUT

Big Idea:

Some processes need energy put IN. Others let energy come OUT.

Endothermic = Energy goes IN (absorbs energy)
Think: "Endo" = "inside" ‚Üí Energy goes inside
Exothermic = Energy comes OUT (releases energy)
Think: "Exo" = "exit" ‚Üí Energy exits

Examples:

Endothermic (Energy IN) Exothermic (Energy OUT)
Ice melting Water freezing
Cooking an egg Burning wood
Cold pack activating Hand warmer activating

üñêÔ∏è Memory Trick:

"Endo = Energy enters" (both start with E)

"Exo = Exits" (both start with Ex)

‚úì Check Understanding:

A hand warmer gets HOT when you activate it. Is this endothermic or exothermic?

See answer

Exothermic! Energy is coming OUT of the hand warmer (that's why it feels hot to you - energy is transferring to your hand).

üìö Step 4: Bonds Hold Things Together

Big Idea:

Chemical bonds are like connections that hold atoms together. Breaking connections requires effort (energy).

üñêÔ∏è Concrete Experience: Magnets

Get two strong magnets and stick them together.

  1. To PULL THEM APART: You need to use effort (put energy in)
  2. When they SNAP TOGETHER: They make a sound (energy released)

Chemical bonds work the same way!

Action Energy Memory Trick
Breaking bonds REQUIRES energy (endothermic) "Break = Take"
Forming bonds RELEASES energy (exothermic) "Form = Warm"

‚úì Check Understanding:

Using the magnet example, explain why breaking bonds requires energy.

"Breaking bonds requires energy because, just like magnets, you need to ________ to pull them apart."
See answer

"Breaking bonds requires energy because, just like magnets, you need to put in effort (do work) to pull them apart."

üéØ Summary: Connect the Ideas

The Full Picture:

  1. Energy is the ability to cause change
  2. Energy can transfer from one thing to another
  3. Endothermic = energy goes IN; Exothermic = energy comes OUT
  4. Breaking bonds = endothermic (takes energy)
  5. Forming bonds = exothermic (releases energy)
