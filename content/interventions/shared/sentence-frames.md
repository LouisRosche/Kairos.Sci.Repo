# Sentence Frames for Science Writing

**Use these frames to scaffold student responses across all tiers.**

Copy relevant frames into form helpText or student page `<details>` elements.

---

## Making Claims (CER: Claim)

**Simple:**
- I think ________ because ________.
- The answer is ________ because ________.

**Intermediate:**
- Based on the data, I claim that ________.
- The evidence suggests that ________.

**Advanced:**
- My claim is that ________, which is supported by ________.

---

## Citing Evidence (CER: Evidence)

**Simple:**
- The [data/diagram/text] shows ________.
- I noticed that ________.

**Intermediate:**
- According to [source], ________.
- The pattern in the data shows ________.
- Evidence from [source] indicates ________.

**Advanced:**
- Multiple pieces of evidence support this claim: first, ________; second, ________.

---

## Explaining Reasoning (CER: Reasoning)

**Simple:**
- This matters because ________.
- This connects to ________ because ________.

**Intermediate:**
- This evidence supports my claim because ________.
- The scientific principle that explains this is ________.

**Advanced:**
- This evidence supports my claim because ________, which demonstrates the relationship between ________ and ________.

---

## Comparing and Contrasting

**Simple:**
- ________ and ________ are similar because ________.
- ________ is different from ________ because ________.

**Intermediate:**
- Both ________ and ________ show ________, but they differ in ________.
- While ________ demonstrates ________, ________ shows ________.

**Advanced:**
- Although ________ and ________ share ________, they differ significantly in ________ due to ________.

---

## Cause and Effect

**Simple:**
- When ________ happens, ________ occurs.
- ________ causes ________.

**Intermediate:**
- Because ________, the result is ________.
- ________ leads to ________ by ________.

**Advanced:**
- The mechanism by which ________ causes ________ involves ________.

---

## Making Predictions

**Simple:**
- I predict ________ will happen because ________.
- If ________, then ________.

**Intermediate:**
- Based on ________, I predict that ________ because ________.
- Given the pattern of ________, I expect ________ to occur.

**Advanced:**
- Drawing on the principle of ________, I hypothesize that ________ will result in ________ because ________.

---

## Asking Scientific Questions (SEP-1)

**Simple:**
- How does ________ affect ________?
- Why does ________ happen?

**Intermediate:**
- What would happen if we changed ________?
- How might ________ influence ________?

**Advanced:**
- What is the relationship between ________ and ________, and how might this change if ________?
- How do changes in ________ affect ________ over time?

---

## Identifying Patterns (CCC: Patterns)

**Simple:**
- I see a pattern: when ________, ________ happens.
- The pattern shows ________.

**Intermediate:**
- The data reveals a pattern of ________ that suggests ________.
- A consistent pattern across ________ indicates ________.

**Advanced:**
- The pattern of ________ across multiple instances suggests an underlying mechanism of ________.

---

## Describing Systems (CCC: Systems)

**Simple:**
- In this system, ________ affects ________.
- The parts work together to ________.

**Intermediate:**
- The system includes ________, ________, and ________, which interact by ________.
- Changes to ________ in this system cause ________.

**Advanced:**
- This system functions through the interaction of ________, where changes to ________ propagate through ________ resulting in ________.

---

## Addressing Misconceptions

**Refutational Frame (g = 0.41 effect size):**

1. Many people think ________.
2. However, this is incorrect because ________.
3. The scientific explanation is ________.
4. We know this because ________.

**Example:**
1. Many people think individual animals evolve during their lifetime.
2. However, this is incorrect because an organism's DNA does not change.
3. The scientific explanation is that populations evolve over many generations through natural selection.
4. We know this because fossils and DNA evidence show gradual changes across generations, not within individuals.

## Station-Specific Frames

### Hook (Phenomenon Introduction)
- This is surprising because I expected ________, but instead ________.
- I wonder why ________ happens when ________.
- This makes me curious about ________.

### Station 1 (Core Concept)
- The key concept here is ________, which means ________.
- I understand that ________ works by ________.

### Station 2 (Data Analysis)
- Looking at the data, I notice ________.
- The trend shows ________, which suggests ________.

### Station 3 (Design/Synthesis)
- My solution addresses ________ by ________.
- I designed ________ because ________.

### Exit Ticket (Integration)
- Connecting this week to Cycle ________, I see that ________.
- Both ________ and ________ involve ________, which shows ________.

---

## Usage Notes

1. **Tier 1 students:** Offer frames as optional support
2. **Tier 2 students:** Require use of at least one frame per response
3. **Tier 3 students:** Provide fill-in-the-blank versions with word banks

**In Google Forms helpText:**
```
SENTENCE STARTERS (choose one):
• "The evidence suggests... because..."
• "Based on the pattern, I predict... because..."
• "This connects to... because..."
```
