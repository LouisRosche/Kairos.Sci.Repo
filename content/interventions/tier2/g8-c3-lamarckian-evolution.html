üîÑ Reteach: Populations Evolve, Individuals Don't

Grade 8 | Cycle 3 | ~15 minutes | Misconception frequency: 45%

ü§î The Confusion

‚ùå What many students think (Lamarckian view):

"Individual organisms evolve during their lifetime."

Examples of this thinking:

  • "The giraffe stretched its neck to reach leaves, so its neck got longer."
  • "The cheetah needed to run faster, so it evolved to be faster."
  • "Whales evolved flippers because they needed to swim."

‚úÖ The scientific understanding (Darwinian view):

"POPULATIONS change over many generations. Individual organisms do NOT change their traits during their lifetime."

The correct explanation:

  • Variation already exists in a population (some giraffes naturally have longer necks)
  • Individuals with beneficial traits survive and reproduce more
  • Over many generations, the population's trait distribution shifts

üÜö Lamarck vs. Darwin: The Key Difference

Aspect Lamarck (WRONG) Darwin (CORRECT)
Who changes? The individual organism The population over generations
When? During the organism's lifetime Across many generations
How? "Need" causes change; traits acquired through use are inherited Natural variation + differential survival/reproduction
Example (Giraffe) Giraffe stretched neck ‚Üí neck got longer ‚Üí passed to offspring Some giraffes naturally had longer necks ‚Üí got more food ‚Üí had more offspring ‚Üí population shifted

‚ö†Ô∏è Language Trap!

Avoid saying things like:

  • ‚ùå "The species evolved to become..."
  • ‚ùå "They developed longer legs because they needed to..."
  • ‚ùå "Evolution gave them..."

Instead say:

  • ‚úÖ "Individuals with longer legs survived more, so over generations..."
  • ‚úÖ "The population changed because those with X trait reproduced more..."
  • ‚úÖ "Natural selection favored individuals that..."

üé≤ The Colored Marble Analogy

Imagine a jar with 50 red marbles and 50 blue marbles (the population).

  • Each marble is "born" as red or blue and stays that color forever (individuals don't change)
  • If you remove 30 random blue marbles (they "die" before reproducing)
  • The remaining marbles "reproduce" (you add more of each color proportionally)
  • After several rounds, the jar has mostly red marbles

The jar's composition changed, but no individual marble changed color!

üìù Worked Example

Question:

A student says: "Whale flippers evolved because whales needed them to swim." Rewrite this using correct evolutionary language.

Step-by-step thinking:

  1. Identify the error: "Needed them" implies purpose-driven change of individuals
  2. Remember: Whales didn't "decide" to have flippers - variation existed
  3. Apply Darwin: Some ancestors had limbs better suited for swimming ‚Üí they survived/reproduced more
  4. Timeframe: This happened over millions of years, across many generations
Answer frame:
"Among the whale's land-dwelling ancestors, individuals with limbs ________ survived and reproduced more. Over ________ generations, the population shifted toward individuals with ________."
Click to see model answer

"Among the whale's land-dwelling ancestors, individuals with limbs better suited for swimming survived and reproduced more. Over millions of generations, the population shifted toward individuals with flipper-like limbs."

Key points:

  • Focus on INDIVIDUALS with pre-existing variation
  • Mention survival AND reproduction
  • Emphasize the time scale (generations, not one lifetime)
  • The POPULATION changed, not individual whales

üéØ Your Turn

Practice Question 1:

Rewrite this Lamarckian statement using correct Darwinian language:

"Cheetahs evolved to run fast because they needed to catch gazelles."

Use this frame:
"In the cheetah population, individuals that were naturally ________ caught more prey and ________. Over many generations, ________."
Check your thinking

Look for:

  • Did you mention pre-existing variation (some were naturally faster)?
  • Did you connect survival to reproduction?
  • Did you emphasize population change over generations?
  • Did you avoid "needed to" or "in order to" language?

Sample response: "In the cheetah population, individuals that were naturally faster caught more prey and had more energy to reproduce successfully. Over many generations, the genes for faster running became more common in the population."

Practice Question 2:

Why can't an individual gazelle "evolve" to run faster during its lifetime, even if being faster would help it survive?

Check your thinking

Sample response: An individual gazelle is born with a specific set of genes that determine its maximum running speed. While the gazelle can train and get stronger, it cannot change its DNA during its lifetime. Evolution requires genetic change, which only happens across generations when certain individuals reproduce more than others. The gazelle's children might have different genes (through mutation or recombination), but the gazelle itself cannot evolve.
