The Disappearing Solid Mystery

Where does a precipitate come from?
Driving Question: How can mixing two clear liquids make something solid appear?

Pour one clear liquid into another - suddenly a white solid appears! Neither liquid contained a solid, yet one forms instantly. Where did the atoms come from? They were rearranged from both liquids in a double replacement reaction!

Station 2: Balancing Equations Lab

20 points | ~15 minutes
Key Rule: Same atoms in = same atoms out. Balancing proves conservation of mass at the atomic level!

Coefficients vs Subscripts

  • Coefficient (2H‚ÇÇO): How many molecules (2 molecules)
  • Subscript (H‚ÇÇO): How many atoms per molecule (2 H per molecule)
  • NEVER change subscripts - that changes the substance!
Balancing Steps: 1) Count atoms on each side. 2) Adjust coefficients only. 3) Re-count to verify. 4) Reduce to smallest whole numbers.
