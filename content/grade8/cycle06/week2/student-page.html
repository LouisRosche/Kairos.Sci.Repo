KEY: This is the ADVANTAGE of electromagnets - you can control them!

The Phenomenon: The Generator Mystery

At a science museum, you crank a handle and a light bulb glows. There are no batteries—just spinning magnets inside the handle.

  • When you stop cranking, the light goes out immediately
  • At power plants, giant turbines spin day and night
  • Somehow this motion becomes electricity in your home 100 miles away

What's the connection between spinning and electricity?

Driving Question: How can spinning a magnet create electricity?

The Big Discovery (1820 - Hans Christian Oersted)

Oersted noticed that a compass needle deflected when placed near a wire carrying electric current. This was REVOLUTIONARY - it proved that moving electric charges create magnetic fields!

Station 2 - Electromagnetic Induction Lab

20 Points | ~15 Minutes

Your Mission: Create Electricity with Magnets

The Big Discovery (1831 - Michael Faraday)

Faraday discovered that a CHANGING magnetic field creates electric current in a wire. This is called electromagnetic induction - and it's how ALL power plants work!

ActionObservationInduced Current
Push magnet INTO coilNeedle deflects RIGHTPositive current
Pull magnet OUT of coilNeedle deflects LEFTNegative current
Hold magnet STILLNo deflectionZERO!
Move FASTERLarger deflectionMore current
Use STRONGER magnetLarger deflectionMore current

THE KEY INSIGHT: Current only flows when there's CHANGE! No motion = no change = no current!

HOME ALTERNATIVE: Use this reference data!

Key pattern to notice:

  • Stationary magnet in coil = ZERO current (no change!)
  • Push magnet in = current flows one way
  • Pull magnet out = current flows opposite way
  • Faster motion = MORE current (more rapid change)
  • This is why generators produce AC (alternating current)!
Need extra support? Click here for explanation hints

Understanding the Pattern:

  • It's not the magnetic field that creates current - it's the CHANGE in field
  • Moving the magnet = changing the field at the coil
  • No motion = no change = no current

Sentence Starters:

  • "When the magnet moves, the field at the coil ___, which causes..."
  • "The galvanometer deflects opposite ways because..."
  • "Power plants need turbines to KEEP spinning because..."

Station 3 - Design an Electric Motor

25 Points | ~20 Minutes (Highest Value!)

Engineering Challenge: Motor vs Generator

Motors and Generators Are OPPOSITES!

DeviceInputOutput
GeneratorMechanical motion (spinning)Electrical energy
MotorElectrical energyMechanical motion (spinning)

Fun fact: They're the SAME device! Run a motor backward and it generates electricity. This is how electric car "regenerative braking" works!

MOTOR COMPONENTS:

  1. Armature - The spinning coil of wire
  2. Permanent magnets - Create stationary magnetic field
  3. Commutator - Switches current direction each half-turn
  4. Brushes - Deliver current to the spinning armature
  5. Power source - Battery or power supply

WHY THE COMMUTATOR IS ESSENTIAL:

Without a commutator, the motor would rotate halfway, then stop (or oscillate back and forth). The commutator reverses the current at exactly the right moment so the magnetic forces keep pushing in the same rotational direction!

  • Current flows → armature becomes electromagnet
  • Electromagnet is repelled/attracted by permanent magnets → starts to rotate
  • At half-turn, commutator flips current direction
  • Field reverses → keeps pushing same rotational direction
  • Continuous spinning!
Need extra support? Click here for design hints

Design Strategy:

  • Permanent magnets on OUTSIDE (stationary)
  • Coil (armature) in the MIDDLE (spins)
  • Commutator attached to armature shaft
  • Brushes touch commutator to deliver current

Sentence Starters:

  • "My motor design has permanent magnets positioned ___ to create..."
  • "The armature is in the center because..."
  • "The commutator reverses current so that..."
