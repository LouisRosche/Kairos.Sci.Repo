Key Concepts Review - Use for Studying!

Week 1: Magnetic Fields & Force-Distance

ConceptKey Point
Magnetic FieldRegion around magnet where force acts; extends in all directions
Field LinesShow direction (N→S) and strength (denser = stronger)
Force-DistanceINVERSE relationship: Double distance = ~1/4 force
PolesLike poles repel (N-N, S-S); Opposite poles attract (N-S)

Week 2: Electromagnetism & Energy Transfer

ConceptKey Point
ElectromagnetsCurrent through coil creates field; More coils + more current + iron core = STRONGER
InductionCHANGING field creates current; No change = no current!
MotorsElectricity → Motion; Commutator reverses current for continuous spin
GeneratorsMotion → Electricity; Same device as motor, run in reverse!

The BIG Connections

  • Electricity → Magnetism: Moving charges (current) create magnetic fields (Oersted, 1820)
  • Magnetism → Electricity: Changing magnetic fields create electric current (Faraday, 1831)
  • They're UNIFIED: Electricity and magnetism are two aspects of one phenomenon: ELECTROMAGNETISM
  • Energy is CONSERVED: It transforms (electrical → magnetic → mechanical) but never disappears

Quick Review: Key Facts for Each Section (Click to expand)

Section A - Magnetic Fields:

  • Dense field lines = stronger field
  • Field lines point N → S
  • Like poles repel, opposite attract
  • Fields have no sharp edge - they fade gradually

Section B - Force Relationships:

  • Inverse relationship: 2× distance = ~1/4 force
  • Graph curves steeply down then levels off
  • This is why refrigerator magnets only work when touching

Section C - Electromagnetism:

  • Stronger electromagnet: more coils, more current, iron core
  • Induction requires CHANGE (motion or varying current)
  • Oersted: current → field. Faraday: changing field → current

Section D - Energy Transfer:

  • Motor: electrical → magnetic interaction → mechanical
  • Generator: mechanical → changing field → electrical
  • Energy is conserved - it transforms, never disappears
