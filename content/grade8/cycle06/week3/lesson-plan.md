# Grade 8 Cycle 6 Week 3 Lesson Plan
## Synthesis & Assessment

**NGSS Standards:** MS-PS2-3 (Primary), MS-PS4-2 & MS-LS2-3 (Spiral)
**Duration:** 2 days (~75 min each)
**Total Points:** 100

---

## Focus
Integration of Weeks 1 & 2 + Spiral from Cycles 4-5

---

## Learning Targets
By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Connect magnetic field properties to electromagnetic principles
2. Apply force-distance relationships to electromagnetic devices
3. Demonstrate understanding of electromagnetism and energy transfer
4. Integrate concepts from Cycles 4-6 in system-level explanations

---

## Day 1: Synthesis & Review

### Opening Routine (5 min)
- Attendance & materials check
- Overview of Week 3 assessment structure
- "Today we'll connect everything about electricity and magnetism"

### Part 1: Synthesis (20 pts | ~15 min)
- **Focus:** Connect W1 magnetic fields + W2 electromagnetism
- **Format:** Short-answer and diagram-based questions

**Synthesis Questions:**
1. Explain how a generator and a motor are related. What's the same? What's different?
2. Draw the magnetic field around a current-carrying wire. How does increasing current affect the field?
3. Why must the armature in a motor keep switching poles? What would happen if it didn't?

**Key Connections to Make:**
| Week 1 Concept | Week 2 Application | Integration |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Magnetic fields | Electromagnet fields | Current creates controllable fields |
| Force-distance relationship | Induction strength | Closer magnet = stronger induced current |
| Permanent magnets | Electromagnetic induction | Both can induce current in coils |

### Spiral Review Station (25 min)
- **Small groups rotate through review stations**
- Station A: Cycle 4 concepts (energy flow, transformation)
- Station B: Cycle 5 concepts (wave properties, EM spectrum)
- Station C: Cycle 6 Week 1 (magnetic fields, force-distance)
- Station D: Cycle 6 Week 2 (electromagnetism, induction)

### Direct Instruction: Assessment Prep (10 min)
- Review assessment format and expectations
- Highlight common misconception traps
- Provide test-taking strategies for constructed explanations

---

## Day 2: Cumulative Assessment

### Part 2: Cumulative Assessment (60 pts | ~40 min)

**Section A: Magnetic Fields (15 pts)**
- Describe field patterns from diagrams
- Predict force direction based on pole arrangement
- Explain field line density meaning

**Section B: Force Relationships (15 pts)**
- Calculate or predict force at various distances
- Explain inverse relationship
- Apply to practical scenarios

**Section C: Electromagnetism (15 pts)**
- Explain how electromagnets work
- Describe electromagnetic induction
- Connect electricity and magnetism

**Section D: Energy Transfer (15 pts)**
- Trace energy transformations in motors/generators
- Explain conservation of energy in EM systems
- Apply to novel scenarios

### Part 3: Misconception Check (20 pts | ~20 min)

**Target Misconceptions:**
| Misconception | Question Type | Correct Understanding |
|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Magnets never weaken | True/False + explanation | Magnets can demagnetize; field depends on factors |
| Electricity consumed | Diagram analysis | Energy transforms, charge conserves |
| E & M unrelated | Multiple evidence question | Unified phenomenon: electromagnetism |
| Only permanent magnets work | Practical application | Electromagnets are controllable magnets |

## MTSS Considerations

### Tier 1 (Universal)
- Field pattern reference sheet
- Motor/generator diagram
- Electromagnetic vocabulary guide

### Tier 2 (Targeted)
- Extended time (up to 1.5x)
- Reduced question load with representative sampling
- Pre-labeled diagrams for analysis questions

### Tier 3 (Intensive)
- Alternative assessment format (oral or project-based)
- One-on-one administration
- Modified rubrics focusing on core concepts

## Post-Assessment

- Identify students needing Tier 2/3 support for Cycle 7

### Intervention Planning
- Schedule targeted reteach for persistent misconceptions
- Create peer tutoring pairs for electromagnetism
