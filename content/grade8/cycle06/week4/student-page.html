COMMON MISTAKE ALERT: "You Can Get More Out Than You Put In"

WRONG: "Gears and pulleys give you free force - you get more energy out than you put in."
RIGHT: "Mechanical advantage trades SPEED for FORCE. You push with less force but for a LONGER distance. Total energy (work) is conserved!"
KEY: There's no such thing as a free lunch in physics - you always trade something!

The Phenomenon: The Tiny Motor Mystery

Your car has a flat tire. You pull out the electric car jack - it's tiny, powered by a small motor the size of your fist. You press a button and watch in amazement as this little motor lifts your 2-ton car off the ground!

  • The motor uses only a few amps of current
  • The car weighs 4,000 pounds (1,800 kg)
  • The motor is smaller than a can of soda
  • It takes about 30 seconds to lift the car

How can such a small motor produce such an enormous force?

Driving Question: How can a tiny electric motor lift a car?

Key Vocabulary This Week

Term Definition
Mechanical Advantage The factor by which a machine multiplies force (MA = Output Force / Input Force)
Gear Ratio Ratio of teeth on driven gear to driving gear; determines speed/torque trade-off
Torque Rotational force - the twisting power of a motor (measured in Newton-meters)
Work Force times distance (W = F x d); measured in Joules
Efficiency Useful energy output divided by total energy input (always less than 100%)
Pulley System Wheels and ropes that change the direction or magnitude of force

Station 1 - Motor Efficiency Investigation

20 Points | ~18 Minutes

Your Mission: Trace the Energy

Motor Energy Flow

Energy flows through a motor system, but not all of it becomes useful work. Let's trace where it goes!

StageEnergy FormWhere Does It Go?
BatteryChemical energyStored in battery chemicals
WiresElectrical energySome lost as heat (resistance)
Motor coilsElectromagneticCreates magnetic field
Motor shaftKinetic (rotational)Some lost to friction
Load liftedGravitational PEUSEFUL OUTPUT!

EFFICIENCY = (Useful Output / Total Input) x 100%

Real motors are typically 70-90% efficient. The rest becomes heat!

HOME ALTERNATIVE: Use this sample data!

Motor Test Data:

  • Battery supplies: 100 Joules of electrical energy
  • Heat from motor: 15 Joules
  • Friction losses: 5 Joules
  • Useful work lifting load: 80 Joules
  • Efficiency = 80/100 = 80%
Need extra support? Click here for hints and sentence starters

Key Concept Reminder:

  • Energy is CONSERVED - it transforms but never disappears
  • "Lost" energy usually becomes heat
  • Higher efficiency means less waste heat

Sentence Starters:

  • "The efficiency is calculated by..."
  • "Energy is lost to ___ at the ___ stage because..."
  • "A more efficient motor would waste less energy as..."

Word Bank:

efficiency • input • output • heat • friction • electrical • kinetic • potential • transform • conserve

Stuck? Click here for step-by-step help

Try these steps in order:

  1. Start with the total energy input (from the battery)
  2. Add up all the "waste" energy (heat + friction)
  3. The useful output = input - waste
  4. Efficiency = (useful output / input) x 100%

Station 2 - Mechanical Advantage Analysis

20 Points | ~15 Minutes

Your Mission: Understand Force Multiplication

Gears: Trading Speed for Force

Gear SetupSpeedForce (Torque)Example
Small drives Big (10:50)5x SLOWER5x MORECar jack, bike climbing
Equal gears (30:30)SameSameJust changes direction
Big drives Small (50:10)5x FASTER5x LESSBike flat ground, fans

GEAR RATIO = Driven teeth / Driving teeth

A gear ratio of 5:1 means 5x more force but 5x slower speed!

Pulleys: Trading Distance for Force

Pulley TypeMAForce NeededRope Pulled
Fixed (1 wheel)1100%Same as lift height
Movable (2 wheels)250%2x lift height
Block & Tackle (4 wheels)425%4x lift height

THE TRADE-OFF: Less force needed = more rope to pull. Work stays the same!

HOME ALTERNATIVE: Practice Problem

Scenario: A car jack needs to lift 4,000 N. The motor produces 40 N of force at its shaft.

  • What mechanical advantage is needed? MA = 4000/40 = 100:1
  • If the motor turns 100 times, how many times does the jack screw turn? 1 time
  • Why does it take 30 seconds to lift the car? Speed is traded for force!
Need extra support? Click here for calculation hints

Mechanical Advantage Formulas:

  • MA = Output Force / Input Force
  • For gears: MA = Driven teeth / Driving teeth
  • For pulleys: MA = Number of rope segments supporting the load
  • Work In = Work Out (Force x Distance is conserved)

Sentence Starters:

  • "The mechanical advantage is ___ because..."
  • "To lift the load with less force, you need to pull ___ more distance because..."
  • "The car jack has a high gear ratio so that..."

Week 4 Summary: What You Learned

Motor Efficiency: Not all energy becomes useful work. Efficiency = Output/Input. Real motors are 70-90% efficient.

Gears: Trade speed for force. Small gear driving big gear = more force, slower speed. MA = Driven/Driving teeth.

Pulleys: Trade distance for force. MA = number of supporting rope segments. Pull more rope = less force needed.

Energy Conservation: Work In = Work Out. You can multiply force but must trade away speed or distance. No free lunch!
