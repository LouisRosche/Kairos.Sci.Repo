What is a Trophic Cascade?

ğŸº Case Study: Yellowstone Wolves

When wolves were removed from Yellowstone (1926):

  • Elk population exploded (no predators!)
  • Elk ate all the willow and aspen trees near rivers
  • Without trees, riverbanks eroded
  • Beavers couldn't build dams (no wood)
  • Fish populations crashed (no beaver ponds)
  • Songbirds left (no trees for nests)

When wolves returned (1995): All of this REVERSED! Even the rivers changed course!

âš ï¸ Learning from Mistakes: The Mongoose Story

Hawaii introduced mongooses to control rats in sugar cane fields. Problem:

  • Rats are nocturnal (active at night)
  • Mongooses are diurnal (active during day)
  • They never even met! Mongooses ate native bird eggs instead.
  • Now Hawaii has a rat problem AND a mongoose problem!

LESSON: Think about ecosystem effects BEFORE introducing biocontrol!
