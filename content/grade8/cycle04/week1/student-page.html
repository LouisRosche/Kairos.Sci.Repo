In the Serengeti ecosystem of Africa:

  • There are millions of pounds of grasses
  • Hundreds of thousands of wildebeest, zebras, gazelles
  • But only a few thousand lions and hyenas

Why are there SO MANY plants and herbivores, but SO FEW top predators? Where does all the energy go?

Key Vocabulary This Week

Term Definition
Trophic Level Position in food chain (1=producer, 2=primary consumer, etc.)
10% Rule Only ~10% of energy transfers between trophic levels
Biomass Total mass of organisms at a trophic level
Energy Pyramid Diagram showing energy decrease at each level (wide at bottom, narrow at top)
Producer Organism that makes its own food through photosynthesis (plants, algae)
Consumer Organism that gets energy by eating other organisms

Where Does Energy Go When a Zebra Eats Grass?

Energy in Grass Eaten: 1000 kcal
Lost to heat (metabolism)~60%
Lost to waste (feces, urine)~25%
Parts not eaten (roots, etc.)~5%
Stored in zebra body~10%

The 10% stored in the zebra is what's available to the NEXT level (lions)!
