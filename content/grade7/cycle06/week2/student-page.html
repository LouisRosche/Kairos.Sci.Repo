Station 1: Seafloor Spreading Evidence

20 Points

In the 1960s, scientists discovered something remarkable: the ocean floor has magnetic stripes! These stripes alternate between normal and reversed polarity, and they're perfectly symmetrical on both sides of the ridge.

Key Vocabulary

Magnetic Reversal
When Earth's magnetic poles switch (north becomes south)
Polarity
The direction of Earth's magnetic field (normal or reversed)
Spreading Rate
How fast new seafloor is created (cm per year)
Mid-Ocean Ridge
Underwater mountain chain where new crust forms

Magnetic Stripe Pattern

As magma cools into rock at the ridge, iron minerals align with Earth's magnetic field‚Äîcreating a permanent record. When the field reverses, stripes alternate!

Magnetic Stripe Symmetry at Mid-Ocean Ridge

Blue = Normal polarity | Red = Reversed polarity | Orange = Active ridge

Notice: Stripes are SYMMETRICAL on both sides!

Seafloor Age Data

Location Distance from Ridge Rock Age
Mid-Atlantic Ridge 0 km < 1 million years

Common Misconception

Wrong: "The ocean floor is ancient‚Äîbillions of years old like the continents."

Correct: The oldest ocean floor is only ~200 million years old. It's constantly recycled‚Äîcreated at ridges, destroyed at subduction zones!

The Mesosaurus Mystery

Mesosaurus was a freshwater reptile that lived ~275 million years ago. Its fossils are found ONLY in Brazil and South Africa‚Äînowhere else on Earth. This creates a puzzle:

  • Mesosaurus couldn't swim across the salty Atlantic Ocean
  • It's found on two continents separated by 5,000+ km of ocean
  • The ONLY explanation: Brazil and Africa were once connected!

Why Multiple Lines of Evidence Matter

Any single piece of evidence might have another explanation. But when multiple independent lines all point to the same conclusion, the argument becomes very strong. This is how science builds confidence!

Station 3: Design a Plate Movement Detector

25 Points

Engineering Challenge

Modern GPS technology can measure plate movement with incredible precision‚Äîdetecting movements of just a few millimeters per year! Your challenge: design a monitoring system to track plate movement across the Atlantic.

Design Constraints

Constraint Requirement
Budget $500,000 for 5-year study
Precision Detect movement to 1 mm/year
Coverage Monitor 3 plate boundaries
Data Required Position, velocity, direction

Available Technologies

Technology Cost Precision Trade-offs
GPS Station (fixed) $50,000 each 1 mm/year Needs clear sky view
Laser Ranging $200,000 0.1 mm/year Expensive, weather-dependent
VLBI (radio astronomy) $150,000 0.5 mm/year Requires multiple stations
Tide Gauges $25,000 5 mm/year Indirect, affected by other factors

Key Question

To measure how the Atlantic is widening, where should you place your monitoring stations?

Answer: You need stations on BOTH sides of the ridge (e.g., one in Brazil, one in Africa) to measure relative movement!

Think About This

Phone GPS: Accurate to ~5 meters

Scientific GPS: Accurate to 1 millimeter‚Äî10,000 times more precise!

If your measurement error is larger than the movement (2.5 cm/year), you can't detect plate motion with a smartphone!
