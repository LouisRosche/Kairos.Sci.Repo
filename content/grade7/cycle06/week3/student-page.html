  • Gentle lava fountains
  • Fluid, fast-flowing lava
  • Gas escapes easily
  • Shield volcano shape (broad, flat)
  • Relatively safe to observe

üí• Plinian Eruption

  • Explosive blasts
  • Thick, slow-moving lava
  • Gas trapped ‚Üí pressure builds

Correct: Eruption style depends on magma properties! Many eruptions are gentle lava flows.

The secret to eruption style is viscosity‚Äîhow thick or runny the magma is. And viscosity is controlled by silica content!

Key Vocabulary

Viscosity
Resistance to flow‚Äîhow "thick" a liquid is
Silica (SiO‚ÇÇ)
A mineral compound that makes magma sticky and thick
Basalt

The Gas Trap Mechanism

All magma contains dissolved gases (water vapor, CO‚ÇÇ). What happens when these gases try to escape?

Magma Type Viscosity Gas Behavior Result
Basalt (low silica) Low (runny) Gas bubbles escape easily Gentle eruption
Rhyolite (high silica) High (thick) Gas trapped ‚Üí pressure builds Explosive eruption!

Station 2: Eruption Type Analysis

20 Points

Different eruption types create different hazards. Understanding these helps communities prepare and saves lives!

Volcanic Hazard Ranking

#1
Pyroclastic Flows

Superheated gas + rock avalanches at 100-700 km/hr, 700¬∞C. Unsurvivable. Killed 30,000+ at Mt. Pel√©e (1902).

#2
Lahars (Volcanic Mudflows)

Hot water + volcanic debris at 60+ km/hr. Can travel 50+ km. Buried Armero, Colombia (23,000 deaths, 1985).

#3
Ash Fall

Heavy, abrasive volcanic ash collapses roofs, damages lungs, grounds aircraft, destroys crops.

#4
Lava Flows

Destructive but SLOW (walking pace). Usually survivable because people can evacuate.

Common Misconception

Wrong: "Lava is the most dangerous volcanic hazard."

Correct: Pyroclastic flows and lahars kill far more people because they're too fast to escape!

Eruption Type vs Hazards

Eruption Type Primary Hazards Why
Hawaiian (effusive) Lava flows, volcanic gases Low viscosity = gas escapes, no explosions
Plinian (explosive) Pyroclastic flows, ash, lahars High viscosity = trapped gas explodes

Station 3: Design a Volcano Monitoring System

25 Points

Engineering Challenge: Mt. Rainier Warning System

Mt. Rainier is a potentially dangerous stratovolcano near Seattle. Over 3 million people live in the lahar hazard zone. Your challenge: design a monitoring system that gives people enough warning to evacuate!

Pre-Eruption Warning Signs

Volcanoes don't erupt without warning. Scientists monitor for these signals:

Warning Sign What It Means Detection Method
Earthquake swarms Magma breaking rock as it rises Seismometers
Ground deformation Magma pushing up from below GPS, tiltmeters
Gas emissions increase Fresh magma releasing gas Gas sensors, spectrometers
Temperature changes Hot magma heating groundwater Thermal sensors, satellites

Mt. Rainier's Special Challenge: Lahars

Mt. Rainier has 25+ glaciers. If an eruption melts this ice, massive lahars will race down river valleys toward Seattle suburbs. Lahars can travel at 60+ km/hr‚Äîpeople may have only 30-60 minutes to evacuate!

Design Requirements

  • Detection: Identify eruption AND lahar formation
  • Speed: Alert system must activate within 5 minutes
  • Coverage: Warn all communities in hazard zone
  • Reliability: Must work 24/7, all weather conditions
