Station 1: Seismic Wave Investigation

20 Points

Seismic Waves: Earth's X-Ray

Earthquakes generate two main types of seismic waves that travel through Earth:

P-Waves (Primary/Pressure)

  • Travel fastest
  • Arrive first at seismic stations
  • Can travel through solids AND liquids
  • Like sound waves (compression)

S-Waves (Secondary/Shear)

  • Travel slower than P-waves
  • Arrive second
  • Can ONLY travel through solids
  • Like shaking a rope side-to-side

The Key Discovery

S-Wave Shadow Zone: Scientists noticed that S-waves disappear on the opposite side of Earth from an earthquake. This means something is blocking them. What could stop S-waves but let P-waves through?

Investigation Steps

  1. Use the seismic wave simulation to trigger an earthquake
  2. Observe P-wave and S-wave travel paths through Earth
  3. Identify where S-waves disappear (the "shadow zone")
  4. Note where wave speeds change (layer boundaries)
  5. Infer what causes the observed patterns
