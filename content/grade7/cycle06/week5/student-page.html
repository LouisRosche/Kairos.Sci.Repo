Part 3: Misconception Check

20 Points | 20 Minutes

Common Misconceptions

This section checks if you've overcome the most common wrong ideas about plate tectonics and Earth's interior. Read carefully!

Misconceptions We Target

Plates Float on Magma?

Wrong: "Tectonic plates float on liquid magma like boats on water"

Correct: Plates rest on the asthenosphere (upper mantle), which is SOLID but flows slowly like plastic over millions of years. S-waves travel through it, proving it's solid!

Continents Move Fast?

Wrong: "Continents move quickly enough to notice in a lifetime"

Correct: Plates move 2-10 cm per year‚Äîabout as fast as your fingernails grow! It takes millions of years to see significant movement.

Earthquakes Are Random?

Wrong: "Earthquakes happen randomly anywhere on Earth"

Correct: About 95% of earthquakes occur at plate boundaries in predictable zones. The "Ring of Fire" is a prime example!

Mantle Is Liquid?

Wrong: "The mantle is liquid magma under the crust"

Correct: S-waves travel through the mantle, proving it's solid. Only small pockets melt to form magma at plate boundaries.
