Review Before You Begin

Week 1: Ocean Acidification

  • CO2 + H2O ‚Üí carbonic acid ‚Üí lower pH
  • pH scale is logarithmic (0.1 = 30% change)
  • Harms shell-building organisms (oysters, pteropods)
  • Conservation of mass: carbon moves through systems
  • Monitoring systems track pH changes

Week 2: Eutrophication

  • Nutrients (N, P) ‚Üí algae bloom ‚Üí die ‚Üí decompose ‚Üí O2 depleted
  • Sources: fertilizers, sewage, animal waste
  • Gulf of Mexico dead zone: 16,000+ km¬≤
  • It's a positive feedback loop
  • Solutions: reduce runoff at the source

Key Vocabulary

Ocean Acidification

pH decrease from CO2 absorption

Eutrophication

Nutrient excess causing O2 depletion

Dead Zone

Hypoxic area with little marine life

Algae Bloom

Rapid algae growth from nutrients

Hypoxia

Low oxygen conditions

Positive Feedback

Change amplifies itself

Carbonic Acid

H2CO3 formed from CO2 + water

pH Scale

Logarithmic measure of acidity
