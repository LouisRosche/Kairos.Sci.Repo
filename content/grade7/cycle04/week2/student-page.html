This Week's Mystery

What happens when nutrients run off into waterways? In 2014, a massive algae bloom turned Lake Erie bright green. 500,000 people couldn't drink their tap water. Fish died by the thousands. How did fertilizer meant to help crops end up poisoning a lake?

By the End of This Week, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Trace nitrogen and phosphorus through ecosystems
  2. Explain the eutrophication cascade (nutrients ‚Üí algae ‚Üí decomposition ‚Üí oxygen depletion)
  3. Analyze real-world data from the Gulf of Mexico dead zone
  4. Design a solution to minimize nutrient runoff

Connecting to Previous Learning

Week 1: You learned how CO‚ÇÇ dissolves in the ocean to cause acidification. This week, you'll see how a DIFFERENT chemical (nitrogen/phosphorus) causes a DIFFERENT problem (eutrophication) through a similar cascade process.

Cycle 3: Remember feedback loops? Eutrophication is another example of POSITIVE feedback - the problem makes itself worse!

üìó Station 1 Help: Nutrient Cycle Modeling

Key Concept Reminder:

  • Nitrogen (N) and Phosphorus (P) are essential nutrients for plants
  • Sources: fertilizers, sewage, animal waste
  • Cycle: Source ‚Üí Runoff ‚Üí Water ‚Üí Algae Growth ‚Üí Death ‚Üí Decomposition

Sentence Starters:

  • "Nitrogen enters the ecosystem when..."
  • "The nutrient travels from ___ to ___ by..."
  • "This is a problem because..."
üìó Station 2 Help: Dead Zone Data Analysis

Data Reading Tips:

  • Dead zone size is measured in square kilometers (km¬≤)
  • Look for TRENDS: Is the zone getting bigger or smaller over time?
  • Compare to reference: Connecticut is ~14,000 km¬≤
  • The Gulf of Mexico dead zone can reach 16,000+ km¬≤!

Sentence Starters:

  • "The data shows that the dead zone has [increased/decreased] because..."
  • "The largest dead zone occurred in ___ and was ___ km¬≤..."
  • "This correlates with nutrient levels because..."
üìó Station 3 Help: Remediation Design

Solution Options to Consider:

  • Buffer strips: Plants along waterways that absorb nutrients
  • Cover crops: Plants grown in off-season to hold nutrients in soil
  • Precision fertilizing: Apply only what crops need, when they need it
  • Wetland restoration: Natural filters for nutrient-rich water

Sentence Starters:

  • "My solution targets [the source/the pathway/the endpoint] because..."
  • "This solution works by..."
  • "A trade-off of this approach is..."

Key Vocabulary

Eutrophication
Process where excess nutrients cause explosive algae growth, leading to oxygen depletion
Dead Zone
Area where oxygen levels are too low for most marine life to survive (hypoxia)
Biogeochemical Cycle
Movement of elements (like N, P, C) through living organisms and the physical environment
Nutrient Runoff
Fertilizers, sewage, or organic waste washing into waterways from rain
Algae Bloom
Rapid, explosive growth of algae when nutrients are abundant
Decomposition
Bacteria breaking down dead organic matter, consuming oxygen in the process
