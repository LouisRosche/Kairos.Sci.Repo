üåä This Week's Mystery: The Green Lake Catastrophe

What happens when nutrients run off into waterways? In 2014, a massive algae bloom turned Lake Erie bright green. 500,000 people couldn't drink their tap water. Fish died by the thousands. How did fertilizer meant to help crops end up poisoning a lake?

ü§î Driving Question: How does fertilizer from farms end up creating "dead zones" in the ocean hundreds of miles away?

üéØ By the End of This Week, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Trace nitrogen and phosphorus through ecosystems
  2. Explain the eutrophication cascade (nutrients ‚Üí algae ‚Üí decomposition ‚Üí oxygen depletion)
  3. Analyze real-world data from the Gulf of Mexico dead zone
  4. Design a solution to minimize nutrient runoff

üîÑ Connecting to Previous Learning

Week 1: You learned how CO‚ÇÇ dissolves in the ocean to cause acidification. This week, you'll see how a DIFFERENT chemical (nitrogen/phosphorus) causes a DIFFERENT problem (eutrophication) through a similar cascade process.

Cycle 3: Remember feedback loops? Eutrophication is another example of POSITIVE feedback - the problem makes itself worse!

üîß Station 3 ‚Äì Design a Remediation Plan

25 Points | ~20 Minutes (Highest Value!)

üîß Engineering Challenge: Reduce Nutrient Runoff

Solution Options to Consider:

  • Buffer strips: Plants along waterways that absorb nutrients
  • Cover crops: Plants grown in off-season to hold nutrients in soil
  • Precision fertilizing: Apply only what crops need, when they need it
  • Wetland restoration: Natural filters for nutrient-rich water
üìó Need extra support? Click here for design hints

Design Strategy:

  • Identify WHERE in the pathway to intervene (source, transport, or endpoint)
  • Consider COST, EFFECTIVENESS, and PRACTICALITY
  • Multiple solutions working together are often better than one

Sentence Starters:

  • "My solution targets [the source/the pathway/the endpoint] because..."
  • "This solution works by..."
  • "A trade-off of this approach is..."

üìñ Key Vocabulary

Eutrophication Process where excess nutrients cause explosive algae growth, leading to oxygen depletion
Dead Zone Area where oxygen levels are too low for most marine life to survive (hypoxia)
Biogeochemical Cycle Movement of elements (like N, P, C) through living organisms and the physical environment
Nutrient Runoff Fertilizers, sewage, or organic waste washing into waterways from rain
Algae Bloom Rapid, explosive growth of algae when nutrients are abundant
Decomposition Bacteria breaking down dead organic matter, consuming oxygen in the process
