The Phenomenon: The Mountaintop Seashell Mystery

Scientists exploring near the summit of Mount Everest made an incredible discovery that seems impossible at first:

  • Marine fossils at 29,000 feet: Trilobites, brachiopods, and crinoids (ocean creatures) embedded in rock near the peak
  • Ancient origin: These organisms lived in an ocean over 450 million years ago
  • Rock type: The fossils are found in limestone - a sedimentary rock that forms in oceans

Driving Question: How can you find seashells on top of a mountain?

Key Vocabulary This Week

TermDefinition
Igneous RockRock formed when magma or lava cools and solidifies. Examples: granite, basalt
Sedimentary RockRock formed when sediments (particles) are compacted and cemented over time. Examples: sandstone, limestone
Metamorphic RockRock formed when existing rock is changed by heat and pressure without melting. Examples: marble, slate
Rock CycleThe continuous process by which rocks transform from one type to another over millions of years
WeatheringThe breaking down of rocks by wind, water, ice, and living things
UpliftThe rising of Earth's crust, often due to plate tectonic forces, exposing buried rocks

Station 2 - Rock Cycle Modeling

20 Points | ~15 Minutes

Your Mission: Trace Rock Transformations

Rock Cycle Pathways

Rocks can transform in MANY directions. Here are some pathways:

  • Igneous → Sedimentary: Weathering breaks down rock into sediments
  • Sedimentary → Metamorphic: Burial provides heat and pressure
  • Any Rock → Magma: Extreme heat melts rock completely
  • Magma → Igneous: Cooling creates new igneous rock
Use the Rock Cycle Explorer - Click process arrows to see transformations!
Need extra support? Click here for hints

Key Concept Reminder:

  • The rock cycle is NOT one-way - any rock can become any other type
  • Two energy sources: Sun (weathering) AND Earth's heat (metamorphism, melting)

Sentence Starters:

  • "Granite can become sandstone by first..."
  • "Plate tectonics drives the rock cycle because..."

THE SCENARIO:

You find a sample of MARBLE on a mountainside. The marble contains fossils of ancient sea creatures.

Your job: Construct a complete timeline showing how this rock formed and reached its current location.

CONSTRAINTS:

  • Timeline must include ALL rock types this material passed through
  • Must explain the transformation process at each step
  • Must include approximate time scales (millions of years)
  • Must explain how it ended up on a mountain
Need extra support? Click here for design hints

Key Clues:

  • Marble is METAMORPHIC rock
  • Marble forms from LIMESTONE (sedimentary)
  • Fossils only form in SEDIMENTARY rock
  • Metamorphism requires heat + pressure (from plate collision)

Timeline Structure:

  • "Step 1: ___ million years ago, organisms lived in..."
  • "Step 2: Their shells accumulated and formed..."
  • "Step 3: The rock was transformed by..."

Week 1 Summary: What You Learned

Rock Types: Three main types - igneous (cooled magma), sedimentary (compressed sediments), metamorphic (changed by heat/pressure)

Rock Cycle: Rocks continuously transform from one type to another - no beginning or end to the cycle

Evidence in Rocks: Rock type tells us WHERE and HOW a rock formed - fossils prove ocean origin

Plate Connection: Plate tectonics drives the rock cycle through subduction, collision, and volcanism
