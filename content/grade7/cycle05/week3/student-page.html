üìó Part 2 Help: Cumulative Assessment

Key Concepts to Remember:

  • Air Mass Codes: m = maritime (wet), c = continental (dry), P = polar (cold), T = tropical (warm)
  • Cold Front: Blue triangles, cold air pushes under warm, rapid storms then clearing
  • Warm Front: Red semicircles, warm air rides over cold, gradual clouds and rain
  • Low Pressure (L): Rising air ‚Üí clouds ‚Üí precipitation
  • High Pressure (H): Sinking air ‚Üí clear skies
  • Close Isobars: Strong winds

Data Analysis Tips:

  • Falling pressure = low approaching = storms coming
  • Rising humidity + falling temp = precipitation likely
  • Wind shift from S to NW = cold front passed

Review Before You Begin

Week 1: Air Masses & Fronts

  • Air mass codes: mT (warm/wet), cP (cold/dry), mP (cold/wet), cT (warm/dry)
  • Cold front: Cold air pushes under warm air ‚Üí rapid storms
  • Warm front: Warm air slides over cold air ‚Üí gradual rain
  • Low pressure (L) = rising air = clouds/storms
  • High pressure (H) = sinking air = clear skies
  • Close isobars = strong winds

Week 2: Data & Forecasting

  • Barometer: measures pressure (falling = storm coming)
  • Radar: shows precipitation location and movement
  • Satellite: views large areas including oceans
  • Pattern: Falling pressure + rising humidity = precipitation likely
  • Wind shift from S to NW = cold front passed
  • Short-term forecasts are more accurate than long-term

Key Vocabulary

Front

Warm Front

Barometer

Isobar
